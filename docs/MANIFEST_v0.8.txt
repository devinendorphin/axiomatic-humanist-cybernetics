      sorries, core-only. v0.8 closes Reviewer #2 findings R2-02
      (Critical) and R2-04 (High): the disclosed episode budget was an
      arbitrary atom never connected to the attestation whose accuracy
      P8 proves, and the four D-R4 disclosure "fields" were untyped
      roles one claim could fill simultaneously.

      Module 4 gains the typed claim language PIOClaim (basis, novelty,
      budget-with-figure, falsif, free: role is type, not content).
      PIOEvent now carries its BudgetAttestation and requires the
      critical set to contain the budget claim bearing THE attested
      figure, the falsifier to occupy the falsif role, and (via
      budget_unique) no conflicting critical budget claim to exist.
      P14: the four D-R4 roles are pairwise distinct claims, by
      constructor disjointness. P15: every compliant register contains
      the budget claim whose figure IS the episode machine's count over
      the attested history — disclosed = attested = computed, one term.
      P16: any budget-role claim in the critical set carries the
      machine's figure. P17: the published figure inherits E1's 72-hour
      bound.
