  ahc-verified-kernel/
      Lean 4 project: 6 source modules, 133 audited theorems, zero
      sorries, core-only. v0.13 adds Module 5 (Seam Ledger / Contested
      Attestation Ledger): the first module addressed not to malformed
      inputs but to WELL-TYPED LIES from legitimate authorities. v0.14
      executes the deprecation scheduled in v0.13: `seamStep` is the
      sanctioned issuing interface, `nakedIssue` is deprecated in-code
      (@[deprecated seamStep]), and L10-L11 prove no unaccompanied bit
      initiates a fresh PIO from ANY episode state (L1' lifted from idle to
      every state, plus its deployment-facing contrapositive). The internal
      estep/EpInput remain -- they are the machine, not the bypass.

      The v0.12 kernel reasons only about what follows GIVEN a trusted
      input's value, never about its truth. `SeamClaim α` wraps a
      load-bearing external input (issue/confirm/novel, the danger
      threshold, certificate validity, criticality, Layer 0 dispositions)
      as a dated, attributed, contestable object carrying an authorization
      chain, evidence refs, a custody-ladder status, warnings-available,
      counter-evidence, a falsification path, an affected-population
      counter-record, conflicts, and a procedural status. The DELIBERATE
      OMISSION is a `truth` field: the module proves NO payload true. It
      does not catch lies; it converts a single administrative utterance
      into a visible multi-party act with preserved contradictions, and
      every theorem is procedural. L3 (step_one_never_implies_step_two)
      is the firewall that keeps the ledger from becoming a new authority:
      a community/ledger trigger yields only step-one acts, never a
      punitive step-two authorization. L5' (seam_trace_stays_inside)
      composes the contestation guard with the v0.12 exposure layer (W21).
      Tier B relations are opaque predicates quantified over every
      valuation (à la Module 3 sensor honesty); no LLM classifier decides
      any protected-path question.
